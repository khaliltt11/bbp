CULTUREKEEPA
2026· 3 Acts· Documentary· Southern Black Identity

CULTUREKEEPA is a feature-length documentary exploring what it means to be young, Black, and Southern in 2026. The film moves through the past, present, and future of Southern Black identity — told entirely through the voices of the people living it.

Rooted in the belief that Southern Black culture is not a footnote — it is a foundation. CULTUREKEEPA exists to document, amplify, and preserve that truth.

Three Acts
I
DA ROOTS
Where did we come from? History, legacy, and the foundations that shaped Southern Black identity.
II
DA NOW
Youth voices speak to the current moment — the pressures, pride, and contradictions of being young and Black in the South today.
III
DA KEEPA
A forward-looking meditation on preservation, ownership, and what the next generation is building.

FRESHMAN SUMMER
2026· Short· Documentary· Pre-Production

Freshman Summer is a documentary short capturing the final chapter of Haywood Middle School's Class of 2026. Cinematic. Verite. A real portrait of what this year meant.

The film opens with a chaotic high-energy sequence before settling into a softer, nostalgic documentary tone — mirroring the emotional arc of finishing 8th grade.

Film Structure
I
TITLE SEQUENCE
Chaotic black-and-white flashing intro. Brat green title card. High energy — sets the tone before the pull-in.
II
THE BRIDGE
Deliberate tonal shift — chaos to warmth. The documentary voice takes over.
III
THE YEAR
The heart of the film. Classmates, moments, memories — the Class of 2026's final chapter.
